Patient is a 2-year-old male referred by his pediatrician for evaluation of multiple congenital anomalies and developmental concerns.

Physical examination:
- Microcephaly: head circumference at the 2nd percentile for age and sex.
- Short stature: height at the 3rd percentile.
- Dysmorphic features including widely spaced eyes, low-set ears, and a broad nasal bridge.
- Clinodactyly of the fifth fingers bilaterally.

Neurological:
- Hypotonia present; the child has poor head control.
- Global developmental delay: not yet sitting independently at 24 months.
- Absent speech; no words produced to date.

Cardiac: A ventricular septal defect was identified on echocardiogram.

Gastrointestinal: Feeding difficulties requiring nasogastric tube supplementation in the neonatal period; now tolerating oral feeds with thickened liquids.

Impression: The constellation of microcephaly, short stature, dysmorphic features, hypotonia, global developmental delay, and absent speech is suggestive of a chromosomal or single-gene disorder. Chromosomal microarray and exome sequencing are recommended.
