Patient is a 4-year-old male referred for evaluation of recurrent seizures and hypotonia.

History of present illness: The patient has experienced multiple episodes of generalized tonic-clonic seizures since age 18 months. Parents report approximately 3-4 seizure episodes per month, each lasting 1-2 minutes. The child also demonstrates significant hypotonia, noted since birth, with delayed motor milestones. He sat independently at 12 months and began walking at 24 months.

Neurological examination: Decreased muscle tone throughout. Deep tendon reflexes are diminished bilaterally. No focal neurological deficits identified.

Developmental history: Global developmental delay is present. Speech is limited to single words. No regression noted.

Family history: No known seizure disorder or neuromuscular disease in first-degree relatives.

Assessment: The combination of seizures, hypotonia, and global developmental delay warrants further genetic and metabolic workup.
