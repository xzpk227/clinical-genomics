Patient is a 6-month-old female referred for evaluation following an abnormal newborn screen result suggestive of a fatty acid oxidation disorder.

Presenting concerns: The infant has had two episodes of hypoglycemia requiring hospital admission. Parents report poor feeding and lethargy during intercurrent illnesses.

Physical examination:
- Hepatomegaly: liver palpable 3 cm below the right costal margin.
- Hypotonia: reduced muscle tone noted on examination.
- No dysmorphic features identified.
- No seizures observed during examination.

Laboratory findings:
- Elevated acylcarnitine profile consistent with medium-chain acyl-CoA dehydrogenase (MCAD) deficiency.
- Liver transaminases mildly elevated.
- Plasma amino acids within normal limits.

Developmental: Age-appropriate development at this time. No global developmental delay identified.

Impression: Findings are consistent with MCAD deficiency. The hypotonia and hepatomegaly are likely secondary to the metabolic disorder. Genetic confirmation via ACADM gene sequencing is pending.

Plan: Dietary management with avoidance of prolonged fasting. Emergency protocol provided to family. Metabolic dietitian referral placed.
