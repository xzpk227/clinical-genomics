Patient is a 14-year-old male with a known diagnosis of Duchenne muscular dystrophy, presenting for annual review.

Musculoskeletal:
- Progressive muscle weakness affecting proximal limb muscles. The patient is now wheelchair-dependent.
- Scoliosis identified on spinal radiograph; Cobb angle measured at 28 degrees.
- Joint contractures present at the ankles and hips bilaterally.
- Muscle atrophy is prominent in the lower extremities.

Respiratory:
- Forced vital capacity has declined to 52% of predicted. Nocturnal hypoventilation is suspected.
- No respiratory failure at rest.

Cardiac:
- Dilated cardiomyopathy confirmed on echocardiogram. Ejection fraction 42%.
- No arrhythmia detected on 24-hour Holter monitoring.

Neurological:
- Intellectual disability is not present; cognitive function is within normal limits.
- No seizures reported.

Plan: Referral to respiratory medicine for consideration of non-invasive ventilation. Cardiology follow-up in 3 months. Physiotherapy to address joint contractures and scoliosis management.
